Intake System Design & Iteration

Co-designed a high-efficiency ground intake for the 2025 season robot. The system integrates a 4-bar linkage deployment mechanism specifically engineered to reliably retrieve 4.5”x11.875” PVC game pieces (GP) off the floor.

Design Evolution

Intake Design V1 Featured an Archimedes screw bottom roller and black compliant wheels (1.5" spacing). This version utilized guide panels and star wheels to push GPs into the indexer.
Intake Design V2 Simplified the mechanism by replacing the bottom roller with a passive ramp. Added smoother internal guide panels and increased wheel density for improved durability and speed.

Technical Analysis

Proof of Concept Early wooden prototyping used to validate roller spacing and bumper clearance. Initial tests confirmed the Archimedes screw's role in orienting perpendicular game pieces.
Guide Panels V1 3D-printed panels located after intake to push GP into the scoring indexer.
// TECHNICAL_NOTICES

- GP that collided with the intake side panels would go up the intake vertically, becoming jammed.
- Archimedes screw roller did not play a big role in orienting GP.
- Teeth on ramp interfered with field carpet.
Guide Panels V2 Implementation of optimized panels resolved the vertical jamming issue. This refinement made the transfer process into the scoring indexer faster and more consistent.
Final Resolution Transitioned to a smoother ramp design. Orientation teeth were removed to prevent field carpet damage while maintaining intaking efficiency.
